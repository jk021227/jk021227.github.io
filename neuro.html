üë• Stakeholders

Stakeholder A: (Neurodivergent individual with ADD)

  • The Emergency contact feature is essential and should be easily accessible.
  • The stimulus overload feature might be less useful since many forget to use it in real-time. Consider using pre-planned alarms for crowded spaces.
  • Notifications should be attention-grabbing to avoid being ignored.
  • The in-game pet is a nice idea, especially if it gives reminders.
  • Avoid asking too many questions when setting up the app to prevent barriers to use.
  • Include features to keep users' hands busy and offer standardized reminders for daily tasks like eating and showering.
  • Avoid language that infantilizes users.

Stakeholder B: (Psychology student with experience working with neurodivergent children)

  • Focus timers and visual reminders are useful but should be optional.
  • The emergency alert feature is crucial, especially for children.
  • The UI should avoid overwhelming users.
  • Offer checkboxes or presets to help users identify specific challenges and customize the app accordingly.
  • Believes the app will positively impact neurodivergent users, aiding social interactions and handling sensory overload.

üìú Product Vision Statement

Ponyo is a mobile application that aims to facilitate communication, centralize wellness/mental health tools, and provide safety measures.
